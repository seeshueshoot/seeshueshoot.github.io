Art For The Masses

Street art is insightful and irreverant, controversial and political, tolerated and banned. Of course I love it!

In 2019, I visited 48 cities and 22 countries in Europe. But in 7 months of cultural exploration, I rarely stepped inside a museum. I ADORE art, but I hate lines, bag checks, and photography bans. My pilgrimage taught me how to love street art, and now I look for it everywhere.

If you really want to learn about a new place, skip the museums and hit the streets. Most cities have local guides and companies that specialize in walking tours. You can ask a local hostel or coffee shop to recommend a neighborhood. Or you can do a simple Google or Instagram search for "street art" plus the location. And you can still visit museums - just save them for rainy days.

15 Reasons Why I Love Street Art

  • the abundance of art that lives in public spaces is impressive - parks, alleys, staircases, government buildings, parking lots and walls of every size
  • likewise the variety - pieces as small as my hand, murals as grandiose as a 10-story building
  • it's coloful and inspiring
  • it has a message
  • street art is like a scavenger hunt for active people - you gotta go looking for it
  • no entrance fee
  • I can take unlimited photos, of whatever inspires me
  • no need to make a reservation or stand in line
  • consuming street art is not limited to certain days and times
  • I don't have to check my bags or coat
  • street art neighborhoods are usually off the beaten path, so I don't have to deal with crowds
  • visiting these smaller neighborhoods supports local artists and businesses
  • and helps me learn the landscape and feel the essence of a place
  • studying local street art gives me insight into the people who live there, and the things they value
  • street art is dynamic and changes constantly (often overnight) - there's always something new to see
